Personal Background

Born and raised in the vibrant state of Rajasthan, India, my fascination with glaciers began during my early education when I was introduced to environmental geography. Growing up in an arid region made my interest in water resources and their conservation particularly poignant.

My journey into glaciology was not a straight path. After completing my undergraduate studies in geography, I found myself increasingly drawn to remote sensing technologies and their applications in environmental monitoring. This interest eventually led me to pursue specialized education in remote sensing and GIS.

A pivotal moment in my career was my first expedition to the Himalayan glaciers during my master's program. The experience of standing on these majestic ice formations, observing their grandeur and fragility firsthand, cemented my commitment to glacier research.

Outside of my academic work, I am an avid photographer, documenting my field experiences and sharing the beauty of remote glacial landscapes with the world. I believe visual storytelling can be a powerful tool for raising awareness about climate change impacts on these critical environments.

I am also passionate about mentoring young researchers and students interested in environmental sciences. Through workshops and field training programs, I aim to inspire the next generation of glaciologists and remote sensing specialists to continue this important work.
