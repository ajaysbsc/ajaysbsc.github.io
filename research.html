Research

Exploring glacier dynamics through innovative research methods

Research Focus

My research focuses on understanding glacier dynamics in the Himalayan region, with a particular emphasis on:

  • ● Glacier mass balance estimation using UAV technology and remote sensing
  • ● Development of frameworks for assessing debris cover thickness on glaciers
  • ● Environmental monitoring in high-altitude regions using satellite imagery
  • ● Field validation techniques for remote sensing data in challenging terrain

Through this work, I aim to contribute to our understanding of climate change impacts on cryospheric environments and develop improved methodologies for monitoring glacier health.

Modeling and Mapping of Supraglacial Debris Cover Thickness using Satellite Remote Sensing Data in selected Glacier(s) of Western Himalayas

Completed

This project developed novel methodologies for accurate estimation and mapping of supraglacial debris cover thickness in Western Himalayan glaciers. By integrating satellite remote sensing data, thermal models, and field validation, the research improved our understanding of glacier melt dynamics in debris-covered areas and contributed to more accurate glacier mass balance assessments.

Unmanned Aerial vehicle-based Monitoring of Polar Ice Sheet near Schirmacher Oasis, Central Droning Maud land, East Antarctica

Completed

Successfully deployed UAV technology for monitoring polar ice sheets in Antarctica, using the Sensefly ebeex drone to collect high-resolution data. Participated in two Antarctic expeditions (43rd and 44th ISEA), focusing on ice sheet dynamics, crevasse detection, and surface feature mapping in the challenging polar environment of Schirmacher Oasis in East Antarctica.

Publications Section

Remote sensing the evolution of debris-covered Panchi Nala-A glacier, India (1971-2021) from satellites and Unmanned Aerial Vehicles

Regional Environmental Change (2023), Volume 23, article number 103

This study investigates the morpho-dynamic evolution of the debris-covered Panchi Nala-A glacier in the Bhaga basin, Western Himalaya, using hybrid remote sensing data from declassified Corona, Landsat, Planetscope, and UAV-based optical images over a 50-year period (1971-2021). Results show glacier shrinkage, terminal retreat, and increasing debris extent, with evidence of glacier thinning in the lower ablation zone.

Authors: Garg, S., Navinkumar, P.J., Godara, A., Sahu, R., Singh, D.K., Ramsankaran, R.
